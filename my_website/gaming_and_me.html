Gaming And Me

When I was a child I received a PS1 for Christmas and from that point I wanted to work in games. I worked out a path in high school in order to pass the subjects I needed to study games in university. Although I enjoyed studying games at university I quickly learnt that the video game industry can be difficult for a designer or manager to break into and that behind the scenes there are many challenges. I still design games and code games as a hobby but I would be more inclined to independently release any games I make instead of joining an existing games company.

My proudest moment in university was creating an audio game as my final project along side my dissertation. I hoped to open games up to those that are visually impaired and also start a discussion about the use of audio in video games as a tool for gameplay.
